Once upon a time in the bustling town of Kottakal, there was a school known for its love of knowledge and its diverse group of students. This school was nestled in a valley, surrounded by towering mountains, which, as legends had it, held the key to understanding the shape of the Earth.

First on the list was Thales, an ancient Greek philosopher. Thales believed that the Earth was flat and floated on water like a gigantic wooden disc. This idea fascinated the students, but they wondered how one could prove such a theory.

Next, they learned about Pythagoras, a brilliant mathematician. Pythagoras speculated that the Earth was a sphere, an idea that intrigued Liam, who loved geometry. He imagined the Earth as a perfect sphere, just like the ones he drew in his math class.

Aristotle, a wise philosopher, believed the Earth was a sphere, too.
